JogStart uses your accelerometer to detect jogging and launch YouTube Music.

Tap below to grant permission (required on iOS 13+).

Android grants this automatically — tap to continue.

How to use

🚀 Quick Start

1Open this page in Chrome on your Pixel 6. Add it to your home screen (3-dot menu → Add to Home Screen) for the best experience.
2Tap ▶ Start Jog Session. The app arms the motion detector and starts the timer.
3Start running. The ring fills green when your cadence is in the jog range (110–210 spm). After 4 confirmed steps at jog pace, the app fires.
4A green music banner appears and a beep plays. Tap the banner to open YouTube Music instantly.
5Tap ■ Stop Session when done, or ↺ Reset to clear all stats and start fresh.

🎵 About Music Launch

Chrome security prevents apps from auto-opening other apps or tabs without a direct user tap. JogStart works around this by:

  • Playing an audio beep the moment jogging is detected (no tap needed)
  • Showing a large tap-to-open banner — one tap opens YouTube Music directly
  • On Android with YouTube Music installed, the OS may offer to open the app instead of the browser

⚙️ Sensitivity Slider

  • 1–2 (Low): Needs strong impact — best if phone is loose in pocket
  • 3 (Medium): Default — works well in armband or hand
  • 4–5 (High): Very sensitive — may false-trigger while walking

💡 Tips

Hold your phone in your hand or use an armband. Don't leave it loose in a bag — step detection is less reliable.

Keep the screen on: set your screen timeout to max before jogging, or enable Screen Wakelock (the app will request it automatically when you start).

The cadence ring color tells you your pace: yellow = walking, green = jogging, red = sprinting.
